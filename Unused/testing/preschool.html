Preschool

When children first enter Tower Day, the main focus is on the child’s social/emotional development.  We socialize children to “life in a classroom”.  The teachers work with children daily on sharing, using words, respecting the other children and the teachers, beginning to take care of their own belongings, and learning to attend to a topic or task at hand.  Within this framework, the teachers use developmental activities to increase cognitive skills, language skills, and fine and gross motor skills. 

Rhyming, playing with word sounds, comparing, opposites, and discriminating are all part of the daily literacy activities in our three year old and four year old classes.  Letter recognition and phonemic awareness games help children get ready for the reading process. Teachers read books to the children everyday as well as help children create their own stories. 

Our preschool children complete all of the math operations that we do as adults.  They collect data, graph, form sets, do simple addition and subtraction, etc.  These are not paperwork activities, but rather hands on activities that are intriguing.  These learning opportunities can take place at the sand/water table, in the block area, while creating paintings or working with manipulatives.
